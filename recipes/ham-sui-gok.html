Fried Glutinous Rice Dumplings (Ham Sui Gok)

Description

Ham sui gok, or fried glutinous rice dumplings, is always on our family's card when we eat dim sum. The wrapper is crispy when you bite in, with a lovely chewy inner layer, and the dumpling filling is juicy and savory. Crispy, chewy, and juicy, all at once? Yes, it's possible, because we pre-cook the luscious filling, use glutinous rice flour for the wrapper to get that chewiness, and finally deep-fry for that delicate crunch.

Ingredients

Wrapper Ingredients

  • 3 oz wheat starch

  • 8 oz glutinous rice flour

  • 4 oz hot water

  • 1.5 oz white sugar

  • 2 oz lard

  • 5 oz cold water

Filling Ingredients

  • 0.50 oz dried shrimp

  • 4 oz pork butt

  • 0.50 tsp salt

  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

  • 2 tbsp water

  • 1 oz dried turnip

  • 0.50 oz dried shiitake mushroom

  • 2 clove garlic

  • 1 oz celery

  • green onion (small amount)

  • cilantro (small amount)

  • 2 tbsp you you tea seed oil

Seasoning

  • 0.50 tsp salt

  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce

  • 2 tsp light soy sauce

  • 0.25 tsp white pepper powder

  • 2 tbsp water

  • 1 tsp cornstarch

  • 1 dash sesame oil

Other Ingredients

  • 3.2 cups you you tea seed oil

Cooking Directions

Step 1: Make dough

  1. Put wheat starch (3 oz) and glutinous rice flour (8oz) into two separate bowls

  2. In bowl with wheat starch add hot water (4 oz) then knead for 30 seconds. Set aside.

  3. In bowl of glutinous rice flour, add lard (2 oz), sugar (1.5 oz), and cold water (5 oz) while stirring.

  4. Once all the water is in, knead the dough for one minute.

  5. Add wheat starch dough piece by piece to the glutinous flour dough, and knead together.

  6. Once dough is sticking together, knead for another minute and a half until the two parts are mixed thoroughly.

  7. Wrap completed dough in plastic wrap and set in refrigerator for one hour.

Step 2: Prepare Ingredients

  1. Rinse dried shrimp (0.50 oz), and then soak in warm water for 8-10 minutes to rehydrate them

  2. Rinse and soak the dried shiitake mushrooms (0.50 oz) the same way.

  3. Mince pork (4 oz), and then marinate it with salt (0.50 tsp), cornstarch (1 tbsp), and water (2 tbsp).

  4. Cut the dried turnip (1 oz) into thin strips, and then dice into small pieces.

  5. Dice the celery (1 oz) the same way.

  6. the dried mushrooms and shrimp are rehydrated, dice both the shrimp and mushrooms.

  7. Peel, mash, and mince the garlic (2 cloves).

  8. Chop the green onion and cilantro into small pieces.

Step 3: Stir-fry filling

  1. Turn stove on high heat and add the You You tea seed oil (2 tbsp).

  2. Stir-fry garlic for 10-15 seconds. Then add the shrimp and stir-fry for 10-15 seconds.

  3. Add the pork, cook for 30-40 seconds.

  4. Add mushrooms, celery, and dried turnip and cook for 40-50 seconds.

Step 4. Season filling

  1. Season filling with salt (0.50 tsp), white pepper (0.25 tsp), oyster sauce (1 tbsp), and light soy sauce (2 tsp).

  2. Stir-fry to incorporate the seasoning into the filling.

  3. Add water (2 tbsp) and cornstarch (1 tsp) to thicken filling. Slowly add until preferred consistency is met.

  4. Stir-fry the filling for another 30-40 seconds, add sesame oil (1 dash), the chopped cilantro and green onions.

  5. Turn off heat and remove filling from stove.

Step 5. Wrap Dumplings

  1. Roll out the dough into thick logs and cut them into equal sized pieces.

  2. Take one piece and form a ball then flatten it down so it becomes a circle.

  3. Press the middle of the circle to create a dip and add a tablespoon of filling.

  4. Pinch the open edges of the dough together over the filling to seal. Take care to wrap tightly around the filling to minimize the amount of air inside.

  5. Rolle the closed dumpling back and forth in your hands to smooth the seam.

  6. Repeat until all dumplings are filled.

Step 6. Deep-fry Dumplings

  1. Pour you you tea seed oil (3.2 cup) into the pot.

  2. Heat oil to 160 degrees Celsius. Then turn off the heat.

  3. Place the dumplings into the oil separately, and cook them until they float to the surface.

  4. When the dumplings float turn the heat to medium and fry for another 10 minutes.

  5. Turn the heat to low to continue frying for the last 3-5 minutes.
